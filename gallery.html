Gallery & Favorite Pieces

A curated collection of pieces that have stayed with me.

Every piece on this list found me at a specific moment: some I learned as a kid, others I heard during a study session, some through a film, some through friends. I've organized them loosely by genre, but honestly, they all blur together.

I've added short notes next to each piece explaining what makes it special to me. N ot formal music analyses, just personal impressions. If you haven't heard something on this list, I'd encourage you to look it up. Most are on YouTube or Spotify within seconds.

The list will keep growing. Piano repertoire is essentially endless, and I'm always finding something new that stops me in my tracks. A strange chord progression, an unexpected key change, a melody so simple it feels like you already knew it.

🎼 Classical

  1. Chopin — Prelude in B Minor (Op. 28 No. 6)
    The piece that made me fall in love with piano. It's tender and melancholy without ever feeling overwrought. The melody sounds like someone searching for the right words.
  2. Debussy — Clair de lune
    Somehow both delicate and enormous. The middle section swells in a way that genuinely surprises me every time, even after hundreds of listens.
  3. Ravel — Pavane pour une infante défunte
    Slow, stately, and impossibly sad. The title translates to "Pavane for a Dead Princess," and it earns that weight without ever being theatrical about it.

🎷 Jazz & Impressionist

  • Bill Evans — Peace Piece
    A single left-hand drone for almost seven minutes while the right hand wanders freely. It shouldn't work as well as it does. It's one of the most meditative recordings I know.
  • Confrey — Dizzy Fingers
    One of the first Jazz pieces I learned, a masterpiece of resonance and bluesy chord progressions. And yes - it leaves your fingers dizzy.
  • Scott Joplin - Maple Leaf Rag (opening)
    The magnum opus of ragtime music. Fun, energetic, fast-paced, yet, intricate enough to hear the emotion of a man composing in the 1910s.
  • Oscar Peterson — Hymn to Freedom
    Joyful in a way that feels hard-won. Peterson's technique is staggering, but this piece is all heart. It moves forward like a deep breath.

🎬 Contemporary & Film

  • Yann Tiersen — Comptine d'un autre été
    From the Amélie soundtrack. Repetitive in the best possible way, and each cycle feels slightly more worn-in and comfortable than the last.
  • Breaking Us in Two - Joe Jackson
    My favorite piano song to play 'kareoke' to. The most creative chord progressions I've heard in a classic rock song.
  • Etta James - At Last (piano reduction)
    My favorite bluesy, slow, cheesy love song that includes some of the most beautiful chord progressions you'll ever hear.
  • Ludovico Einaudi — Experience
    Minimalist and sweeping at the same time. I've listened to this on long train rides and it makes everything outside the window look cinematic.
