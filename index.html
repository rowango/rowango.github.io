About This Site

Piano music has been a constant companion throughout my life. Since I began playing at 7, piano has become my greatest stress reliever. Whether I'm studying late at night, taking a walk, or just sitting quietly, there's always a piece playing somewhere in the back of my mind. This site is my attempt to put words — and images — to that feeling.

I don't stick to one genre. One afternoon I might be lost in a Chopin prelude, moved by the way it breathes and pauses like a conversation. The next morning I'll wake up with a Bill Evans Jazz chord stuck in my head, so dense and warm it feels like sunlight. And then there are the classics I grew up with: Billy Joel, Elton John, and Paul McCartney are all magicians on the keys.

What draws me to piano specifically is its intimacy. A single performer, 88 keys, and an enormous range of emotion. You can hear the pedal lift, the breath between phrases, and the tiny hesitations a musician makes when they lean into a passage they love. It's one of the few instruments where the performer's personality is almost impossible to hide.

This site collects my favorite pieces across classical, jazz, and contemporary music. On the Gallery page you'll find a curated list with short notes on why each piece matters to me. I hope something here resonates with you, or better yet, introduces you to a piece you've never heard before.

There's nothing quite like the sound of a grand piano in a quiet room.
